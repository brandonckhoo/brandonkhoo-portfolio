Go1: Content Intelligence and Discovery Strategy

Open loop diagnosis and 12 month target architecture.

Before: Today's Open Loop

Go1 has content, search, and recommendations. What it does not yet have is the intelligence loop needed to match the right learning to the right learner at the right time.

1 · Content Intelligence
Content understanding is incomplete.

Metadata enrichment is patchy. Quality, freshness, and contextual tagging are still largely manual.

Go1 does not yet have a systematic intelligence layer for understanding what content is, how good it is, and where it fits.

2 · Discovery
Search is weakly personalised.

Search combines semantic and keyword retrieval, but relevance logic is still rudimentary.

Learner role, compliance context, and organisational priorities are not deeply reflected in ranking. The burden of relevance still sits with the learner.

3 · Recommendations
The model learns from weak signals.

Recommendations are trained mostly on enrolment-level signals rather than learning quality.

The system cannot reliably distinguish between assigned learning, useful learning, and ignored learning.

4 · Business Impact
Humans absorb the gap.

Admins spend too much time curating manually. Learners are left to find things themselves and often do not.

Confidence in Go1 erodes as content overwhelm increases.

⊗
The loop is open. Completion, skip, assessment, and admin override signals do not reliably flow back into ranking. The system does not improve with every learning decision.
After: 12 Months. The Closed Intelligence Loop

Go1's intelligence flywheel: content informs discovery, discovery drives recommendations, outcomes feed it all back in.

8 · Recommendations
Outcomes improve the next recommendation.
Voluntary completions, assessment scores, and compliance outcomes outweigh clicks. The model retrains on quality signals, not activity volume.
7 · Learner Experience
Recommendations reach learners in context.
Recommendations surface in the homepage feed, search results, and via Morgan in Slack and Teams, at the moment they matter, not buried in a catalog.
6 · Admin Experience
Human in the loop. Admin steers and approves.
AI shortlists content matched to the team's role and compliance context. The admin approves, swaps, or overrides. Every action feeds back into the system.
5 · Discovery and Search
Recommendations become visible and trustworthy.
Every recommendation surfaces with a reason: role match, compliance deadline, or peer completion rate. Admins and learners can agree, dismiss, or override with context, not blind trust.
1 · Content Intelligence
Go1 enriches content independently of providers.
Provider metadata is an input, not the source of truth. An AI enrichment pipeline using Claude Sonnet 4.6 (quality and role relevance scoring on top 5K content) and Claude Haiku 4.5 (bulk classification across 9.8M items) generates consistent signals across skill coverage, quality, freshness, and role relevance. Go1 owns and controls this layer.
2 · Content Intelligence
Learner and org context becomes usable.
Role, team, compliance obligations, and learning history are already in Go1 via HRIS integrations. A learner profile service stores this context in Azure Redis Cache for low-latency retrieval at ranking time, using data that already exists.
3 · Discovery and Search
The catalog narrows to what is actually in scope.
Elasticsearch semantic search narrows 130,000 courses to a relevant candidate set using learner, role, and compliance context. Not just the keyword typed.
4 · Discovery and Search
Best options prioritised, not just most popular.
A ranking model scores candidates on role relevance, content quality, freshness, and outcome history. XGBoost provides an interpretable baseline that improves decision quality without requiring a mature ML platform from day one.
↺
When this loop runs well: admins spend less time curating, learners find relevant content without searching, compliance coverage improves, and Go1 earns more trust with every learning decision. The goal for 12 months is not a perfect system. It is a materially better one that gets smarter over time.
