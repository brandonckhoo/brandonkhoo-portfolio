## The one thing to stretch into for the next conversation

The panel noticed that when you hit a hard question, you tended to reach back to what you've done at Amplitude to anchor your answer. That experience is genuinely valuable and we're not asking you to set it aside. But the Guardian round is specifically designed to assess curiosity — how you engage with the unknown, not just how you apply the known.

Go1's context is different from Amplitude's in some important ways: B2B organisational-led learning, a buyer who isn't the learner, compliance as a primary driver rather than engagement, and an admin layer that sits between the system and the outcome. The strongest signal you can give in this conversation is showing that you're actively curious about those differences — asking questions, surfacing tensions you haven't resolved yet, and being willing to say "I don't know how this works here yet, and here's what I'd want to find out."

The Guardian round isn't looking for polished answers. It's looking at how you think when you're outside your comfort zone. Lean into that.
