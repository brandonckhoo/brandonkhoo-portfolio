Go1: What We Are Actually Fixing

Current architecture vs. target architecture and the closed-loop recommendation system

Current State: Broken Pipeline
Content Providers (250+)
Over-tag broadly to maximise exposure. Metadata is gamed.
↓
Content Library (170K+ courses)
Sparse, unreliable metadata. No quality or freshness signals.
↓
Search (Semantic + Keyword)
No personalisation. Ranks content, not people.
↓
Recommendations Engine
Trained only on enrolments. Measures intent, not outcomes.
↓ ↓
Learner
Generic feed. Ignores recommendations. Searches manually.
Admin
Watches courses end to end. Curation burden is enormous.
No signals flow back. The system never learns. The loop is broken.
Target State: Closed Loop
Go1 Content Intelligence
Skills, quality, freshness, role fit
Learner Profile
Role, compliance, HRIS data
Behavioural Signals
Completions, skips, feedback
↓ ↓ ↓
Recommendation Engine
Trained on engagement quality, not enrolments
↓
Personalised Discovery
"Why this was recommended" visible on every surface
↓ ↓
Learner
Relevant feed. Completes courses. Leaves feedback.
Admin
Reviews a shortlist, not a library. One-click assign.
Engagement signals feed back into the engine. The loop closes. The system gets smarter.
What We Are Actually Fixing (3 Root Causes)
1. Content Intelligence Layer
Before

Provider-supplied metadata is gamed. No quality scoring. No freshness signals. No role relevance mapping.
After

Go1 owns the metadata layer. AI enriches content from first-party analysis, independent of provider tags.
2. Training Signal
Before

Model trained on enrolments only. Optimises for clicks, not learning. Measures intent, not outcomes.
After

Model trained on completion rates by role, skip signals, and explicit feedback. Optimises for outcomes.
3. Learner and Org Context
Before

System has no structured knowledge of who the learner is, what role they are in, or what they need to comply with.
After

HRIS-linked role profile, compliance obligations, and learning history inform every recommendation surface.
