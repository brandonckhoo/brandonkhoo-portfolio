# Microsoft PM Case Study: AI-Powered Product Mockup Ingredient

**Company:** Microsoft
**Type:** Take-home case study
**Status:** In progress

---

## The Exercise

Shape an AI-powered product mockup feature for a fictional website builder (similar to Shopify, Wix, Squarespace).

The product serves **small businesses and creators** who are:
- Non-technical
- Have limited creative skills
- Want professional-looking results without specialist tools or expertise

**The capability:** AI-powered product mockups — users generate high-quality visuals of their products "in context" (e.g. on a desk, in a home, in use) without professional photography.

---

## What They're Evaluating

They want to see **good judgment, scoping, and clarity of thought** — not a complete spec.

### 1. Assumption Framing
- Who are the primary users?
- What are they trying to achieve?
- What makes this hard or intimidating today?

### 2. Outputs — Quality & Success Definition *(core part)*
- What does "good" look like for AI-generated product mockups?
- How would you define and assess output quality consistently?
- What does success look like (metrics, signals, user feedback)?
- They want a **clear point of view on quality**, not just measurement approaches.

### 3. Experience — AI-Level Judgment
- Where should AI be opinionated vs. flexible?
- What controls, defaults, or constraints are essential for novice users?
- What established creation patterns would you avoid recreating, and why?
- Goal: re-imagine creation in an **AI-first model**, not AI layered onto expert workflows.

### 4. V1 Scope & Shaping Process
- How would you get from broad idea → tightly scoped v1?
- Rough scope for v1 (experience/capabilities + outputs unlocked)
- At least one trade-off decision made for v1
- They want to see **the process**, not just the outcome.

---

## Deliverable Formats (pick what's relevant)

- Hands-on testing of existing AI tools/models
- UX flows, prototypes
- Example prompts, eval ideas, or quality rubrics
- Target metrics or leading indicators
- Workshop or decision-making techniques (assumption mapping, prioritization, scoping exercises)

---

## Not in Scope

- Deep user/market research
- Validation on desirability, viability, feasibility
- Detailed product spec
- Roadmap or expanded problem space
- Polished UI mocks, tech architecture, engineering breakdown, GTM plan
- Polished slide deck — they want **raw work**

---

## Format & Time

- Any format (slides, doc, FigJam, live prototype walkthrough, notes)
- Strong response = ~2–3 hours of focused work
- Goal: problem → solution hypothesis as rapidly as possible
- They value **thinking, rapid experimentation, forming strong opinions** — not polish

---

## Follow-Up Questions (prep for interview)

**Clarify (pick 1–2):**
- What are you optimizing for in v1?
- What should be explicitly avoided in v1?
- What's your top risky assumption, how would you validate it?
- "Good" is subjective — how do you approach defining it?
- Can you describe the overall system architecture or data flow?
- What user feedback are you expecting at v1 launch?

**Stress test — constraints, failure modes, tradeoffs (pick 1–2):**
- What if users only have bad phone photos?
- What if outputs look inconsistent across multiple products?
- What if it takes ~30s to generate an image?
- What if website visitors complain about AI images?

**Deep dive — quality/evals/controls (pick 1–2):**
- What if users ask for more control?
- What if users get overwhelmed?
- Why not (or why) a chat UX?
- Is there another product with a UX you'd compare to?

**Scale & boundaries (pick 1–2):**
- A new image model drops — how do you assess whether to use it?
- This feature isn't unique — how would you differentiate?
- If users love it — what do you do next?
