# The Future of How We Ship

> We've entered the phase where all products will look more or less the same.

The AI wave has commoditised the interface layer. Given the same foundation models, similar design systems, and shared infrastructure tooling, the surface area of any two competing products is narrowing fast. Feature parity is no longer a moat — it's table stakes.

The question is no longer *what* you ship. It's what makes you worth keeping.

---

## The three things that will separate generational companies

### (a) Research that underpins your claims

When the output looks similar, trust becomes the differentiator. Companies that invest in deep, proprietary research — whether that's behavioural data, longitudinal studies, or domain-specific benchmarks — can make claims that competitors cannot. Research is the foundation of credibility, and credibility compounds.

Quality of output follows directly from the quality of input. If your model, your product, or your workflow is built on a richer understanding of the problem than anyone else has, the gap shows up in the result — even when the interface looks the same.

### (b) Quality of output

Accuracy, reliability, and trust are not features you add. They are the product. In an environment where every team can ship a working prototype in a weekend, the companies that win are the ones where the output is consistently better — fewer errors, tighter reasoning, more useful responses.

This is hard to fake and hard to copy. It requires investment in evaluation, iteration loops, and honest measurement. Most teams skip this because it's slow. That's exactly why it's a moat.

### (c) Distribution: how you reach and expand revenue

Self-serve, enterprise, partners, strategics — distribution mix is a strategic decision, not just a sales motion. The companies that build multiple channels early create compounding revenue quality that is difficult to replicate. A self-serve base funds experimentation. An enterprise layer validates credibility. A partner ecosystem multiplies reach without headcount.

Revenue quality matters as much as revenue quantity. Diversified distribution is harder to build but far harder to displace.

### (d) How much automation you have

Automation is leverage. The teams that automate their own workflows — from research synthesis to onboarding to support to QA — operate at a different velocity than those that don't. This isn't about replacing people. It's about removing the ceiling on what a small, focused team can ship and maintain.

The best products will increasingly be built by teams that dog-food their own AI capabilities. If you're shipping automation tools but running manual processes internally, you're leaving signal on the table.

---

## The formula

```
[Product research] + [Revenue quality] + [Automation] = Generational company
```

These three inputs are not independent. Research improves output quality, which improves conversion, which funds the automation that accelerates the next research cycle. The loop compounds.

The companies that understand this early — and build the infrastructure to support all three simultaneously — are the ones that will be standing when the interface layer fully commoditises.
